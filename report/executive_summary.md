# Executive Summary
## 10-Year Savings & Investment Plan for a New College Graduate
**Prepared for:** Burton Hollifield, Tepper School of Business, CMU — Spring 2026  
**Date:** April 7, 2026

---

## Who Is the Client?

A 22-year-old college graduate entering the labor market in May 2026 with a $65,000 starting salary, $30,000 in federal student loans, and an ambition to accumulate **$150,000 in real (inflation-adjusted) purchasing power** within 10 years as seed capital for a home down payment.

---

## The Recommendation

> **Allocate investable savings to a mean-variance optimal portfolio of 60% IVV (S&P 500), 13% USMV (low-volatility equity), and 27% SHV (short-term Treasuries). Rebalance annually.**

This allocation—derived from maximizing the Sharpe ratio on a 5-asset universe and then calibrating risk exposure to the client's stated 20% drawdown tolerance via the Capital Allocation Line—is the strategy the analysis unambiguously selects.

---

## Key Numbers at a Glance

| Metric | Recommended Strategy | All Equity (IVV) | Do Nothing (SHV) |
|--------|---------------------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Probability of reaching $150k goal | **61.6%** | 64.3% | 0.0% |
| Median terminal real wealth | **$157,068** | $168,672 | $102,523 |
| 5th-percentile floor | **$122,760** | $91,085 | $98,184 |
| Annualised Sharpe ratio | **0.871** | 0.640 | 0.000 |

The recommended strategy nearly matches All Equity's goal probability while **raising the worst-case floor by $31,675**. A client who goes all-equity to chase the 3pp extra probability takes on a floor that is $31k lower. Mean-variance theory explains why that is a bad trade.

---

## Why This Strategy Wins

1. **Factor tilts improve efficiency.** Adding USMV (β = 0.72 vs market) to IVV raises the portfolio Sharpe from 0.70 (IVV/AGG/SHV tangency) to 0.87, without increasing expected risk.

2. **The emergency buffer is free.** The 27% SHV allocation is not dead weight—it serves as the client's 6-month emergency fund ($21,000 at Year 1) and earns the risk-free rate (~1.5% annualised) while staying liquid.

3. **Savings trajectory is tight but feasible.** Deterministic cashflow modelling shows contributions rising from $6,093 in Year 1 to $16,914 in Year 10, totalling $123,359 in undiscounted contributions. Reaching $150k real requires modest positive returns—broadly achievable under historical data.

## Biggest Risks to the Plan

| Scenario | P(Goal) drops to… | Action |
|----------|-------------------|--------|
| Equity crash at Year 3 | 37.3% | Hold course; do not sell (behavioural discipline) |
| Job loss at Year 3 | 2.2% | Rebuild emergency fund before resuming contributions |
| Persistent 4.5% inflation | 16.9% | Increase savings rate; consider I-bonds allocation |

---

## One-Line Takeaway

Own the market efficiently, tilt toward low-volatility to soften the left tail, keep a liquid buffer— and save consistently for 10 years. Everything else is noise.
